Research

Zero-Day Detection with Machine Learning

PI: Professor Ian Flood

Institution & Department: University of Florida, College of Design, Construction and Planning

Affiliation: Aug 2024 – Present

Research Focus:

In an era where our digital footprints are larger and more vulnerable than ever, zero-day attacks represent one of the most significant cybersecurity threats. My research focuses on leveraging advanced machine learning algorithms to enhance Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), enabling real-time detection of previously unseen vulnerabilities and attack patterns. By moving beyond traditional signature-based detection, this work aims to develop adaptive AI models that continuously learn from evolving network traffic and user behavior to proactively identify emerging threats.

Research Overview & Methodology:

  • Evaluate various machine learning models to determine which are most effective at detecting zero-day anomalies in network traffic.
  • Design and train deep learning models on known network behaviors, then adapt them to identify previously unseen anomalous patterns indicative of zero-day exploits.
  • Compare AI-driven detection performance against traditional IDS and SIEM systems to demonstrate improvements in detecting unknown threats.

Project Responsibilities:

I am responsible for developing the model training pipeline, curating datasets of simulated malicious and benign traffic, and implementing anomaly detection algorithms. My role also includes evaluating model performance, fine-tuning hyperparameters, and preparing research findings for dissemination.

Significance of the Research:

Zero-day attacks exploit vulnerabilities unknown to developers and security teams, making them invisible to conventional detection methods. This research aims to close that gap by building adaptive, AI-powered security solutions capable of identifying threats in real time, significantly reducing the risk of successful cyberattacks.
